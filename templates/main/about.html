About Social Stories

What are Social Stories?

Social Stories are short descriptions of social situations that help individuals understand and respond appropriately to various social contexts. They were developed by Carol Gray in 1991 and have become a widely recognized tool for supporting individuals with autism spectrum disorders and other social learning challenges.

Key Benefits:
  • Understanding: Help individuals comprehend social situations and expectations
  • Preparation: Reduce anxiety by providing advance information about what to expect
  • Learning: Teach appropriate social behaviours and responses
  • Independence: Promote self-confidence in social interactions
  • Communication: Provide a common language for discussing social situations
How Social Stories Work

Social Stories use specific criteria to describe social situations in a way that is meaningful and accessible to the reader. They typically include:

Descriptive Sentences

Define where situations occur, who is involved, what they are doing, and why.

Perspective Sentences

Describe the feelings, thoughts, and reactions of others in the situation.

Directive Sentences

Suggest appropriate responses or behaviours for the situation.

Affirmative Sentences

Express commonly shared values or opinions within a culture.

Research & Evidence

Social Stories are evidence-based interventions that have been extensively researched and proven effective for individuals with autism spectrum disorders and other social learning differences.

Studies have shown significant improvements in social understanding, appropriate behaviour, and reduced anxiety when Social Stories are used consistently.

Who Can Benefit?
  • Individuals with autism spectrum disorders
  • People with social anxiety
  • Children learning social skills
  • Adults navigating new social situations
  • Anyone seeking to understand social expectations
